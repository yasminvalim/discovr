Tecnique - New Order

Technique New Order is a British electronic music band formed in 1980. They emerged from the post-punk scene in Manchester, England, and became pioneers of the synth-pop and electronic dance genres. The band was comprised of Bernard Sumner, Peter Hook, Stephen Morris, and Gillian Gilbert.

Their debut album, "Movement," released in 1981, showcased their unique blend of guitars and synthesizers.

It was heavily influenced by their previous band, Joy Division, and marked the beginning of their transition from a post-punk sound to a more electronic direction.

In 1983, Technique New Order released their breakthrough album, "Power, Corruption & Lies." It featured the iconic track "Blue Monday," which remains one of the best-selling 12-inch singles of all time. The album showcased their innovative use of drum machines, synthesizers, and catchy melodies, solidifying their status as electronic music pioneers.

Throughout their career, Technique New Order continued to push boundaries and experiment with different sounds and styles. They seamlessly integrated electronic elements with rock instrumentation, creating a distinctive sound that influenced generations of musicians.

In addition to their musical contributions, Technique New Order's iconic album artwork, often designed by Peter Saville, became synonymous with their brand. The band's visual aesthetic and innovative use of graphic design played a significant role in shaping the image of the post-punk and electronic music scenes.
